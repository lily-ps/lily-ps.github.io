  <h2>What I do</h2>
  <p>
    I am a PhD student working on coastal rewilding. My research uses environmental data,
    spatial analysis, and AI to build a rewilding potential map in Europe.
  </p>

  <p>
    I want to better understand what drives coastal ecosystem degradation,
    and how these systems can recover and reorganize over time through nature-based solutions,
    with minimal human intervention.
  </p>

  <h2>Why I do this</h2>
  <p>
    Coastal ecosystems are under pressure from climate change, biodiversity loss,
    and long histories of human intervention.
  </p>

  <p>
    Better ways of understanding recovery can help support restoration
    and rewilding efforts.
  </p>

  <h2>Challenge and goal</h2>
  <p>
    Coastal rewilding is still not clearly defined, and there is ongoing debate
    about what it really means in practice.
  </p>

  <p>
    My goal is to contribute by making it more measurable and better understood,
    using data and quantitative approaches.
  </p>

  <p>
    Step by step, I aim to build tools that support decision-making with
    transparent and data-driven evidence.
  </p>
