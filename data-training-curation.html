Curation & Training

What is curation?

Curation is the process of checking whether deposited data are complete, understandable, and reusable. It is not only about storing files. It is about making sure that data can actually be interpreted and used by someone else in the future.

Why use curation in training?

One of the easiest ways to practice preparing a good deposit is to look at data from the other side — the side of the curator. Many repositories will curate your data before keeping it for 5 to 20 years on their platforms.

In this training, participants evaluate someone else’s deposited dataset using a curation form provided by Recherche Data Gouv.

Source (in French):
https://recherche.data.gouv.fr/en/category/33/guide/curation-report-template

Translated version (English):
https://github.com/lily-ps/lily-ps.github.io/blob/main/Curation_Exercise.zip

The idea is simple: By reviewing a dataset as a curator would, it becomes much easier to understand what makes data complete, clear, and reusable. The curation form can also be used as a checklist before clicking "publish" on your data.
