Coastal rewilding · Data integration · AI
PhD Researcher at Nantes Université & Syddansk Universitet

Summary

I work at the intersection of environmental data, spatial analysis, and artificial intelligence to map rewilding potential across European coasts, mostly on three habitats: mudflats, saltmarshes, and seagrass meadows.

My research explores why coastal ecosystems degrade, and how they can recover and reorganize through nature-based solutions (NbS). I am particularly interested in low-intervention approaches, where ecosystems are given the space and conditions to regenerate naturally.

Motivation

Coastal ecosystems are crucial in climate regulation, biodiversity support, and shoreline protection. Yet, they are rapidly degrading due to climate change, biodiversity loss, and long histories of human pressure.

Coastal rewilding remains not fully defined, with ongoing debates around its meaning, scope, and implementation.

My goal is to make rewilding more measurable, operational, and data-driven by developing quantitative frameworks and models to assess rewilding processes.

By the end of my PhD, I aim to deliver decision-support tools, including a coastal rewilding map and an analytical framework grounded in data and designed to be reusable and extensible by others.

Open Science & Data Practices

I am interested in Open Science and research data management, focusing on reproducible workflows, documentation, and transparent sharing of code and methods.

I am trying my best to make my work accessible, reusable, and aligned with FAIR principles.
