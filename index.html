Lily Puspasari

Coastal rewilding · Data integration · AI
PhD Researcher at Nantes Université & University of Southern Denmark

Summary

I work at the intersection of environmental data, spatial analysis, and artificial intelligence to map rewilding potential across European coasts, with a focus mostly on three habitats: mudflats, saltmarshes, and seagrass meadows.

My research explores why coastal ecosystems degrade, and how they can recover and reorganize through nature-based solutions (NbS). I am particularly interested in low-intervention approaches, where ecosystems are given the space and conditions to regenerate naturally.

Motivation

Coastal ecosystems are crucial for climate regulation, biodiversity support, and shoreline protection. Yet, they are rapidly degrading due to climate change, biodiversity loss, and long histories of human pressure.

Despite growing interest, coastal rewilding is still not fully defined, with ongoing debates about its meaning, scope, and implementation.

My goal is to make rewilding more measurable, operational, and data-driven. I develop quantitative frameworks, models, and hypotheses to better define and assess rewilding processes.

By the end of my PhD, I aim to deliver decision-support tools, including a rewilding map and an analytical framework grounded in data and designed to be reusable and extensible by others.

Open Science & Data Practices

Alongside my research, I am particularly interested in Open Science and research data management. I focus on building reproducible workflows, documenting data processes, and sharing code and methods in a transparent way.

I aim to make my work accessible, reusable, and aligned with FAIR principles, contributing to a more open and collaborative scientific ecosystem.
